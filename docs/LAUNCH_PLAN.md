# MyNaavi — Launch Plan

**Last updated:** 2026-04-27
**Status:** Pre-Beta. V56.4 build 113 in Internal Testing.

---

## 1. Positioning

> *"You don't forget because you don't care. You forget because life is full.
> The biggest thing you don't have is time. Work, family, health, the house, the small things that won't wait — they all compete for the same hours. The list you carry in your head grows because there's no time to put each item where it belongs. The result is stress — and important things slipping through."*

**Category:** AI agent for time-poor adults. **Not** a senior-care product. **Not** hardware. Pure software.

**Audience:** Busy professionals, family caregivers managing multiple schedules, small-business owners, anyone whose mental load exceeds available hours. Seniors are one segment, not the segment.

**Promise:** Naavi does the small things — texts, reminders, calendar coordination, message drafts, reservations, follow-up emails — so the important things don't slip through.

---

## 2. Peer products worth studying

| Product | Why study |
|---|---|
| **Granola** / **Plaud** / **Limitless** | AI capture + orchestration. Turn meetings, calls, life into structured action. Closest to Naavi's Record-a-visit + extract-actions flow. |
| **Reflect** / **Mem** | Self-organizing personal knowledge with AI. Time-poor professionals dump everything in; AI surfaces relevant items. Closest to Naavi's REMEMBER + Global Search. |
| **Motion** / **Reclaim** / **Sunsama** | AI calendar that schedules tasks across life domains. Auto-resolve-conflicts logic and context-aware scheduling are mature. |
| **Personal.ai** / **Tab** / **Friend** | "Your AI memory" positioning. Marketing language and onboarding worth studying — same time-poverty + cognitive offloading angle. |
| **Lindy** / **Cluely** / **Adept** | Pure AI agent products. Their action orchestration UI (cards + draft + confirm) parallels Naavi. |
| **Notion AI** / **Sana** | Knowledge layer + agent on top of personal data. Worth comparing how they price for individuals vs. teams. |

**What to extract:** time-poverty messaging, cross-tool orchestration UX, action confirmation patterns, billing model, capture-to-action latency.

**Drop from study:** ElliQ, GrandPad, Pillo, Carenote, Sunbird — wrong category (hardware / senior-only).

## 3. Six prep tracks

| # | Track | Blocks Closed Beta? | Blocks Open Launch? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Stability watch on personal phone — 1 week, no new severe bugs after V56.4 | ✅ | ✅ |
| 2 | Privacy + data-handling page — calendar, email, contacts, location, health data; retention; deletion | ✅ | ✅ |
| 3 | One-page "What MyNaavi does for you" + 60-sec video. Lead with the time-poverty quote. | ⚠ helpful | ✅ |
| 4 | Onboarding flow — first 60 seconds must show one orchestration action ("text my wife I'll be late," "remind me to call Sarah at 4 PM," "what's on my plate today"). No menu tour. | ⚠ helpful | ✅ |
| 5 | Pricing model — free during Beta. Decide before open launch whether subscription, freemium, or one-time. | ❌ | ✅ |
| 6 | Support inbox `hello@mynaavi.com` already routes to founder; commit to 24h reply during Beta. | ❌ | ✅ |

## 4. Closed Beta — next 2-4 weeks

**Target:** 10–20 invitees who feel the time-poverty problem.

| Profile | Count |
|---|---|
| Busy professionals (full-time + family + commute) | 4–6 |
| Small-business owners / self-employed | 2–3 |
| Family caregivers managing multiple people's schedules | 2–3 |
| Tech-comfortable curious testers (edge-case finders) | 4–6 |
| Geographic mix | half local Ottawa, half remote |

**Single weekly email asking:** *"What did MyNaavi do that saved you a few minutes? What did you have to do twice because MyNaavi missed it?"*

**Shared bug list** visible to testers (Notion or Google Doc).

---

## 5. Open Beta — after Closed proves clean (2-3 weeks later)

- Soft launch via mynaavi.com signup form (already exists).
- Cap at 100–300 users.
- Track 3 (one-pager + video) + Track 4 (onboarding) must be done.
- **Press targets** (productivity / busy-professional outlets, NOT senior outlets):
  - *The Verge*, *Lifehacker*, Show HN
  - Productivity newsletters: Nat Eliason, Tiago Forte's *Building a Second Brain*, Khe Hy's *RadReads*
  - Local-Ottawa angle: CBC Ottawa, Ottawa Citizen — only when there's user testimony to back it

---

## 6. Open launch — 2-3 months out

- Pricing on, billing wired, support mature.
- Wider press push.
- **Partnerships:** small-business productivity platforms, time-management coaches, "second brain" knowledge-worker communities.

## 7. Social media + SEO strategy

| Platform | Priority | Why | Commit? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Twitter/X** `@mynaavi` | Active | AI/tech audience; fastest reach for "Show what Naavi did today" posts | YES |
| **LinkedIn** company + personal | Active | Busy professionals = target. Time-poverty messaging resonates. Backlink gold. | YES |
| **mynaavi.com/blog** (or Substack) | Active | Long-form content is THE SEO lever. Each post earns backlinks. | YES — highest SEO return |
| **YouTube** | Reserve handle | Hosts onboarding video + future demos. Video transcripts boost SEO. | Reserve, post sparingly |
| **Reddit** account | Reserve handle | Organic-only posting in r/productivity, r/adhd, r/parenting, r/smallbusiness. Don't spam. | Reserve, comment occasionally |
| **Product Hunt** | Reserve handle | Launch event when open-Beta ready. | One-time |
| **Hacker News** | Reserve account | Show HN post when stable. | One-time |
| **TikTok / Instagram Reels** | Skip unless committing to short-video pipeline | Otherwise time sink | Skip for now |

**SEO truth:** social profiles themselves don't rank. **Backlinks from blog posts, press mentions, podcasts, partner sites do.** Social is for amplification, not ranking.

**Active commitment:** 3 platforms only — Twitter/X, LinkedIn, blog.

**Reserve handles** on the rest with consistent name `mynaavi` (no underscore, no dot, no MyNaavi vs my-naavi mix).

---

## 8. What to avoid

- Paid ads or press push **before** a full week of clean closed-Beta usage.
- Senior-care framing in any marketing copy — boxes the product into the wrong category.
- Comparing to ChatGPT/Claude/Pi in marketing — they're general; Naavi is *an agent that does life chores*. Different positioning, different price tolerance, different word-of-mouth.
- Spreading social effort across 7+ platforms before traction. Pick 3, do them well.
