## What Naavi is, in one paragraph

Naavi is a voice assistant built for this time in life — when the number of small things you're asked to hold in your head has quietly grown longer. Warranties, prescriptions, birthdays, appointments, the email you meant to deal with Thursday night and forgot. She reads your calendar, your email, your saved files, and she remembers what you tell her. When she sends anything on your behalf, she reads it back and waits for you to say yes. She is yours.

---

## Three ways to reach her

**1. In the MyNaavi app, by typing.**  
Open the app, tap the message box, type, send. Like a text message.

**2. In the MyNaavi app, by speaking.**  
Open the app, tap the green microphone button (bottom left), speak, let go. She listens and replies.

**3. By phone call.**  
Save the MyNaavi phone number in your contacts. Call it like any other number. She picks up. You talk. You hang up when you're done.

All three give you the same Naavi. She remembers what you told her in one channel when you reach her through another.

## Your first five minutes — one-time setup

### 1. Sign in with Google

Open the MyNaavi app. On the welcome screen, tap **Sign in with Google** and pick your Google account. This is the step that connects Naavi to your calendar, your email, and your Google Drive — nothing else in the app will work until you sign in.

Once you're signed in, tap the **⚙️ Settings** icon at the top right of the home screen to finish the rest of setup.

### 2. Your name

Type your first name. Tap *Save name*. This is what Naavi will call you.

### 3. Your phone number

Type your number in international format, like `+1 613 555 1234`. Tap *Save*. This is how Naavi calls you for morning briefs and reminders.

### 4. Home address

Scroll down to *Location alerts*. Type your home street address (e.g., `123 Main St, Ottawa, ON`). Tap *Save home address*. This lets you say things like *"alert me when I arrive home"* and have Naavi know where "home" is.

### 5. Work or office address (optional)

If you have a regular workplace or office, add it too. Then *"when I arrive at the office"* will work the same way.

### 6. Morning brief call

Scroll to *Morning Brief Call*. Turn it **On**. Pick a call time (default 8:00 AM). Tap *Save*. Naavi will call you every morning at that time with your day in one paragraph — schedule, weather, priority emails, reminders.

### 7. Location permission (only if you want arrival alerts)

On the *Location alerts* card, tap *Manage location alerts*. A permission page appears. Tap *Allow location*. When Android asks, choose **Allow all the time**. This is how Naavi knows to fire an alert when you arrive somewhere.

**That's it for setup.** You're ready to talk to her.

## Ten things to try on your first day

Pick any — say them out loud on a voice call, or type them in the app.

### 1. Ask about your schedule

> *"What's on my calendar today?"*  
> *"Do I have anything Thursday afternoon?"*

### 2. Create an event

> *"Put Layla's hockey practice Tuesday at six on the calendar."*  
> *"Book my dentist Thursday at 2 PM."*

### 3. Set a reminder

> *"Remind me to call Sarah this Friday at 10."*  
> *"At 5 PM today, remind me to take my pills."*

### 4. Remember something for later

> *"Remember that the garage door opener code is 4471."*  
> *"Save that my insurance policy number is AB-884120."*

### 5. Find something in your email

> *"Pull up the last invoice from Bell."*  
> *"When did Canadian Tire last service my brakes?"*

### 6. Ask about a person

> *"What do I know about David Chen?"*  
> *"When is my sister's birthday?"*

### 7. Send a text or email on your behalf

> *"Text my daughter I'll be 20 minutes late."*  
> *"Email my lawyer to confirm the meeting Thursday."*

Naavi drafts the message, reads it back, and waits for your *"yes"* before sending. Nothing leaves the phone without your approval.

### 8. Set a location alert

> *"Alert me when I arrive at Costco."*  
> *"Text my wife when I leave the office."*

### 9. Set a weather alert

> *"Text me at 7 AM if rain is forecast tomorrow."*  
> *"Alert me if it snows in Ottawa this weekend."*

### 10. Record a conversation, like at the doctor

On a phone call with Naavi, at the start of your doctor's visit:

> *"Naavi, record my visit."*

She records. She stays quiet. At the end:

> *"Naavi, stop."*

By the time you reach your car:
- A full written summary is in your email.
- The conversation transcript is saved to your Drive.
- Any medication changes she heard are on your calendar.
- Any follow-up appointments are booked.

## What to expect when she replies

**She keeps it short.** One or two sentences, most of the time. If she needs more, she'll ask.

**She reads back before she sends.** Every message — SMS, WhatsApp, email — she reads back to you first and waits for *"yes"*. If she got it wrong, just tell her what to change.

**She says "from your saved locations" or "from Settings" when she pulls from memory.** That way you know when she's using a saved value vs. finding something new.

**She asks before she guesses.** If she doesn't know which Costco you mean, she asks. If after a few tries she still can't find the place, she says *"please check the exact location and call me back"* rather than setting something wrong.

---

## Your privacy

- **Your data is yours.** Nothing you tell Naavi is shared with anyone. There is no dashboard your children or anyone else can watch.
- **Nothing is sent without your confirmation.** Every outgoing SMS, WhatsApp, or email is read back first.
- **Your location is used only to fire alerts you set up.** No history of where you've been is stored.
- **Your files stay in your Google Drive.** Naavi reads them with your permission; she doesn't move them or share them.
- **You can turn anything off in Settings.** Location, morning call, Naavi herself.

## What to do when something goes wrong

Nothing works right every time, especially this early. If something feels off:

1. **Try rephrasing.** Say the same thing a slightly different way.
2. **Try typing instead of speaking** (or vice versa). Sometimes the text path works when voice misunderstands a name or number.
3. **Say "cancel"** if Naavi seems stuck waiting for a yes.
4. **Screenshot it and send to me.** Reply to my invitation email with a screenshot or a quick description.

Your "rough edges" reports are what make Naavi better. Don't worry about sounding polite — I'd rather hear "this didn't work" ten times than not at all.

## The morning call — what to expect

If you turned it on, at your chosen morning time your phone rings from the MyNaavi number. Pick up. In one paragraph, Naavi gives you:

- Today's calendar.
- The weather where you are.
- Two or three priority emails that landed overnight.
- Any reminders or bills due soon.
- A closing *"Anything else?"* — you can ask a question right there and keep the conversation going.

If you miss the call, she'll try again. If you miss all three attempts, the brief is saved as a document in your Drive so you can read it later.

## Quick reference — the Settings screen

| What | Where | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| Your name | Settings → Your Name | What Naavi calls you |
| Phone | Settings → Your Phone Number | How Naavi calls you |
| Home address | Settings → Location alerts → Home address | What "home" means in alerts |
| Work address | Settings → Location alerts → Work address | What "office"/"work" means in alerts |
| Location alerts | Settings → Location alerts → Manage | Permission for arrival/departure alerts |
| Morning call | Settings → Morning Brief Call | Daily wake-up briefing call |
| Sign out | Settings → Sign Out | Log out; your data stays, you just stop using the app |
