## Notes for all five scenario scripts (1, 3, 4, 5, 6)

- **One actor, one day:** all four consumer stories can be filmed in a single day with one actor (man, 65-75, warm, ordinary) moving through four sets: kitchen, car, driveway, hallway. Saves cost.
- **Phone on screen:** show the phone close enough that Naavi's voice is clearly the phone's, not a disembodied narrator. No avatar face for Naavi — voice only.
- **Consistent voice:** all Naavi dialogue should use the **same** Deepgram `aura-hera-en` rendering, or the **same** recorded voice from a single call. Multiple voices across videos confuses viewers.
- **No action music.** Soft ambient background. Silence around her lines. Seniors respond to calm pacing; rushed-edit reels feel like ads.
- **Always caption.** Both Robert's side and Naavi's side. ~80% of social feed views are sound-off.

# AI video platforms — what they're good at, what they want from you

Landscape as of early 2026. Prices and features change fast; verify before committing.

## By job

### A. Text-to-video (scene generation from a written prompt)

Best for: scripts 1, 3, 4, 5, 6 — where you need moments of real life (kitchen, car, waiting room) that don't exist as footage.

| Tool | Strengths | Limitations | Typical input |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Runway Gen-3 Alpha** | Cinematic quality, camera-move control, "act one" lip-sync feature | 10-s max per clip (stitch multiple), character consistency is moderate | Text prompt + optional image reference |
| **OpenAI Sora** | Highest fidelity, longer clips (up to ~60 s), good physics | Limited availability, expensive, tight content policy (especially faces) | Text prompt, optional image/video seed |
| **Google Veo 2 / Veo 3** | Strong for everyday realistic scenes, good camera work | Narrower access, enterprise pricing | Text prompt |
| **Pika 2.0** | Fast, affordable, good for ideation | Lower fidelity than Runway/Sora, ~10 s max per clip | Text prompt, optional image |
| **Luma Dream Machine** | Cheap, fast, good motion | Noticeable AI "feel" on faces | Text prompt |
| **Kling AI 1.6** | Very good at people, longer clips (up to 3 min), affordable | Chinese platform — data/privacy review needed for brand use | Text prompt |

**Gotcha for our scripts:** every one of these is best at 5-10 s SHOTS. Build your script shot-by-shot (exactly the table format I gave you), generate each shot, then stitch in a video editor (CapCut, Descript, DaVinci Resolve).

**Biggest challenge across all T2V tools:** the same actor looking the same across multiple shots. Your options:
1. Use Runway's character-reference feature (upload a portrait, re-use across shots).
2. Record the actor ONCE on a phone, then use AI only for B-roll (inserts between the real shots).
3. Accept minor inconsistencies — viewers rarely scrutinise if the story lands.

### B. Avatar-based presenter video (studio explainer with a talking head)

Best for: script 2 — the 90-second "How Naavi works" narrated demo.

| Tool | Strengths | Limitations | Typical input |
|---|---|---|---|
| **HeyGen** | Best lip-sync, 500+ avatars, multilingual, easy editor | The avatar will never be Wael unless you pay for the custom-avatar tier (~$30 + 2 min of video of Wael) | Script + avatar choice + voice |
| **Synthesia** | Most mature, agency-quality look, big avatar library | Stiffer than HeyGen's latest, pricier | Script + avatar + voice |
| **Colossyan** | Good for scenarios (multiple avatars in one scene) | Smaller library | Script + avatar(s) |
| **D-ID** | Photo → talking head (upload a photo of Wael and have it speak) | Quality varies, ethical care when animating real photos | Photo + text + voice |

**For Wael narrating script 2:** record a 2-minute video of yourself talking, upload to HeyGen's "Instant Avatar" or D-ID's "photo avatar", then you can type ANY script and produce presenter-style content with your face on any language and tone. Effectively a cloned-presenter workflow.

## Requirements checklist — prepare once, reuse

To hand to any of the above, you'll want these assets ready:

1. **Script tables** — the ones in this doc (each row is one shot; paste rows straight in).
2. **Visual reference** — 2-3 images of the kind of actor and setting you want (e.g., a photo of a warm-looking older man in a kitchen). Most T2V tools accept an image "style reference."
3. **Character reference image** — a clean portrait shot for the actor so the tool can keep the face consistent across shots (Runway, Pika both accept this).
4. **Voice files for Naavi** — 10-15 s of her saying the lines in each script. Recorded from a live call or generated via Deepgram Aura.
5. **Voice files for Robert** — either the actor's own voice (if real filming), ElevenLabs clone of Wael, or a stock male mature voice.
6. **Logo PNG** — the MyNaavi wordmark in both dark and light variants.
7. **Caption styling** — font (suggest: Inter Semibold or DM Sans), size (48-point for landscape, 56-point for vertical), colour (white-on-soft-black-scrim).
8. **Aspect ratios** — generate each shot 3 times: 16:9 (1920×1080), 9:16 (1080×1920), 1:1 (1080×1080).

## A pragmatic first step

If you want to ship something *this week* without hiring a production:

1. **Book a local actor for 3 hours** — one afternoon covers five scenes (kitchen, car, driveway, hallway, waiting room) with a single performer.
2. **Film on an iPhone 15/16 Pro** in 4K 24fps. Quality is more than enough.
3. **Use real Naavi voice** by calling the product during the shoot and recording her lines directly.
4. **Edit in CapCut** (free, mobile-capable) or **DaVinci Resolve** (free, desktop) using the scripts as the edit decision list.
5. **Use Runway or Pika** ONLY for B-roll animations (calendar icons filling in, folders opening) — not the human shots. That's where AI is actually useful today; the human shots still look uncanny.

Total cost for 5 consumer scripts + 1 narrated demo: roughly $500-1500 depending on actor day rate. Much less than most assumed AI-only budgets, with a dramatically better output.
