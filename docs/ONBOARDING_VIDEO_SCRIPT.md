# Naavi — Onboarding Video Script

**Purpose:** A 90-120 second video that introduces a new user (or visitor) to what Naavi is, what it already knows, and 3 concrete things to try. Goes on the `how-to-use.html` page and the in-app first-launch screen.

**Tone:** warm, unhurried, like a kind friend explaining how something works. **No tech-bro energy.** Target audience reads at their own pace.

**Visuals:** clean, light cream background (matches the new website theme); phone-mockup demos for action shots; user shown as silhouette (privacy + universality).

**Duration target:** 90 seconds. Break-even point for completion rate on marketing video research.

---

## Storyboard (10 scenes, ~9 sec each)

### Scene 1 — Hook (0-9 sec)
**Voice-over (VO):**
> "You don't forget because you don't care. You forget because life is full."

**Visual:**
Cream background. Words appear typed in your warm Lora serif. Final word "full" lingers. Subtle teal accent under the word.

**No music** — silence emphasizes the words.

---

### Scene 2 — What is Naavi (9-18 sec)
**VO:**
> "Naavi is a voice assistant that handles the everyday details — appointments, reminders, the loose ends that pile up — so you don't have to carry them in your head."

**Visual:**
Phone illustration in frame (clean line art, like the icons on mynaavi.com). A user silhouette speaks; speech bubbles flow into the phone; the phone organizes them into clean categorized cards.

**Music:** soft acoustic intro fades in.

---

### Scene 3 — Day one knowledge (18-30 sec)
**VO:**
> "From your very first call, Naavi already knows your name, your contacts, your home, your work, and the people you talk about most. No setup. No teaching."

**Visual:**
A grid of 4 cards animates in:
- 👤 Your name
- 🏠 Home address
- 📒 Contacts
- 📅 Calendar

Each card glows briefly as it appears, then settles.

---

### Scene 4 — Try it: calendar (30-39 sec)
**VO (Robert's voice — warm older male):**
> "What's on my calendar today?"

**Naavi (Aura Hera-style female voice):**
> "You have three things — coffee with David at ten, the dentist at one, and dinner with Sarah at seven."

**Visual:**
Phone screen shows the user speaking, then Naavi responds with a clean 3-line answer. Animated text reveal in sync with the voice.

---

### Scene 5 — Try it: leave time (39-48 sec)
**VO (Robert):**
> "What time should I leave for the dentist?"

**Naavi:**
> "About 25 minutes from home — leave around 12:30."

**Visual:**
Map view in phone, location pin drops on the dentist, route line draws from home, departure time pops up in teal.

---

### Scene 6 — Try it: remember (48-57 sec)
**VO (Robert):**
> "Remember Sarah's birthday is April 15th."

**Naavi:**
> "Got it. I'll remind you a few days before."

**Visual:**
A note slides into a folder labeled "Memory." Then a calendar event appears in April with a small 🎂 icon.

---

### Scene 7 — Naavi learns you (57-69 sec)
**VO:**
> "Every conversation, Naavi gets to know you better. The names you say often. The questions you ask most. Over the first week, the answers you ask for daily come back in seconds."

**Visual:**
Show a clock counting down "Day 1: 8 sec" → "Day 7: 3 sec" for the same question. Subtle improvement bar fills in teal underneath.

---

### Scene 8 — Privacy (69-78 sec)
**VO:**
> "Nothing you say is shared. No dashboard your children can watch. Every message is read back before it sends — nothing leaves until you say yes."

**Visual:**
Phone shows a "Send to Sarah?" confirmation modal. User says "yes." Message flies away. Privacy lock icon flashes.

---

### Scene 9 — Closing thought (78-87 sec)
**VO:**
> "You shouldn't have to remember everything yourself. Now you don't."

**Visual:**
Cream background returns. Naavi logo (teal) fades in centered. The hero punch line from the website appears below: "You forget because life is full."

---

### Scene 10 — Call to action (87-95 sec)
**VO:**
> "Try it now. Call Naavi at one, two-four-nine, five-two-three, five-three-nine-four."

**Visual:**
Phone number appears large, in teal. "MyNaavi.com" subtle below. Music resolves softly.

---

## Production notes

**Voice talent:**
- VO narrator: warm female, 40s-50s, neutral North American
- Robert: male, 60s, calm and deliberate (NOT actor-y; Robert is real, not performing)
- Naavi (in-scene voice): use Aura Hera output directly — gives the brand its real voice

**Music:**
- Acoustic guitar / soft piano. No drums. No build. Sits under, never on top.
- Recommend custom from a library like Musicbed or Artlist; avoid YouTube Audio Library (overused).

**Pacing:**
- Generous pauses. Don't rush. The audience is meant to feel calm.
- Average reading pace ~150 wpm; slow to ~135 wpm for the punch lines.

**Color palette (matches mynaavi.com):**
- Background: warm cream `#FAFAF7`
- Card background: slightly darker cream `#F2EFE8`
- Accent: teal `#5DCAA5`
- Text primary: `#1C1C1E`
- Text secondary: `rgba(28,28,30,0.72)`

**Typography:**
- Headlines: Lora (serif), bold weight
- Body: Inter (sans), regular weight

## Where this video lives

1. **Hero of `how-to-use.html`** — embedded above the fold (after the page H1)
2. **First-call optional play** — if the user is on a smartphone with the app, prompt: "Want a 90-second tour?" → plays the video
3. **Email signature in onboarding email** — first email after signup includes link
4. **Social media** — short version (60-sec cut) for Instagram/TikTok; full for YouTube + LinkedIn

---

## Production budget guidance

- **DIY (you record VO + I/AI generate animation):** $0-200 — uses tools like Synthesia or AI animation libraries
- **Mid-tier (freelance video editor + your VO):** $800-1,500
- **Professional (full studio production):** $3,000-6,000

For a v1 that you can iterate on, the **DIY or mid-tier** is fine. You can re-shoot scenes individually as the product evolves.

---

## Variants

- **30-sec teaser** for ads — uses scene 1 + scene 4 + scene 10 only
- **Robert-perspective version** — first-person, all from Robert's POV, no animation, just the phone screen as he talks. More intimate; great for trust-building.
- **Caregiver version** — addresses the adult-child watching parent's phone use. Different emotional register: "Mom won't have to remember the appointments anymore."
